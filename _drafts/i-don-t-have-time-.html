I remember I was 16, prepping up for the finals which would help me get a spot into the University. School in the mornings, studying in the afternoons, after school classes in the evenings and then basketball practice at nights. It was the first time I start thinking that I don't have time to do anything else.

In the University I had all the time of the world but still it was not enough. All day in the class, projects, midterm exams, finals and go on.

And then I got my first real job, teaching students Unix bash scripting and working for more than 20 hours per week. Still, no time!

And then I started to consistently contribute to Open Source projects like the Fedora project and co-founded Serres Linux User Group. Still, no time!

You get the picture, there was no more free time, right? Well, I am not sure anymore.

Only after 10 years I get to realize that there was always time. But there was time only for the things that I wanted, I loved and I cared about.

Let's run an experiment.

Coffee anyone?

Will you have a coffee with me in three weeks from now, on Tuesday at 18:00.

What do you say? Well most, of the people will say that they don't know or they don't have the time or to talk again in two weeks.

Something that I have learned to trust over the years is my calendar. And quite recently I managed to surpass "my fear" of scheduling something in 4 months ahead.

There is a reason for being in my calendar and I am going to do it.

Priorities

The key for trusting your calendar is to learn to plan your day/week/month according to your priorities.

We all have different priorities but we all struggle to prioritize them based on their importance and our desires.

What would keep you away from scheduling a coffee in three weeks from now? Are you afraid of staying late in the office or generally that something might change your plans the last moment?

[caption id="attachment_758" align="alignnone" width="1440"] "It's not impossible". As found on the walls of Patras Hackerspace[/caption]

Sir yes sir!

It's time to start putting a little bit more thinking into our planning process.

Is it important? Is it something you would like to do? Alright then, let's schedule it!

If you have something else planned for that day, it's fine! It's not a problem turning people down because you have other plans.

We will dive more into the prioritization process and how to be on top of your everyday life in another blog post.

Today it's all about:

  1. Taking decisions based on your priorities
  2. Surpassing the fear of planning ahead and
  3. Learning to trust your calendar
